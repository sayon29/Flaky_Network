The architecture uses an event-loop driven sender built with `poll()` to decouple packet redundancy from the strict 20ms harness clock. Instead of relying on the optional reactive feedback channel—which incurs a fatal Round-Trip Time (RTT) penalty—the system uses proactive, time-staggered duplication. The sender transmits a primary packet at t=0 and schedules a duplicate echo at t=1ms (tuned for Profile B) to establish a race condition that beats the network's delay spikes.To strictly adhere to the 2.0x bandwidth cap, the sender drops the echo on 1 out of every 20 frames, resulting in an exact 2.00x overhead. The receiver maintains a simple boolean `seen` array to immediately forward the first valid arrival of any sequence number and silently drop late duplicates. The 81ms delay is the theoretical mathematical floor for Profile B, calculated by adding the 1ms sender offset to the absolute maximum 80ms network jitter. The system's success relies entirely on the joint probability of independent network drops lowering the overall miss rate below the 1.0% threshold. Consequently, the design will break if the network's maximum delay spike increases by even 1ms, as the 1ms staggered echo would arrive at 82ms and miss the deadline. It will also fail if the network's baseline packet loss exceeds ~10%, because the joint probability of both independent packets dropping (0.10 * 0.10) equals 1.0%, instantly violating the strict deadline-miss cap.
